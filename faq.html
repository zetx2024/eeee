Frequently Asked Questions

An initiative by Young Researcher, Youth Research Journal is the first journal published by high school students, for high school students.

We aspire to promote young researchers and writers by walking them through the standard procedures of publishing a research article; that is, delivering a manuscript, editing & peer-review, and finally acceptance or rejection. Through our monthly-issues, we hope to bring the hard work of those writers to light while also providing a constant stream of quality, entertaining, and highly scrutinized content for readers hungry for explore. These articles, however, are but an initial spark that encourages readers to go beyond and delve more deeply into their topics of interest on their own, advertising independent research and self-learning.

Every year YRJ, host 3 flagship program Winter, Spring, and Summer Research Program.

To participate, all you need to do is fill in a quick registration form and follow the required guidelines.

YRJ is unique in that participants can research any topic they want! Researchers can choose a topic theyâ€™re passionate about, something they wish to dive deeper into, or something they want to study in university. The only limit is the extent of their creativity and imagination.

The assessment form is where we ask students to share their initial research topic with us. No one will be disqualified based on the assessment form, it is simply the time by which participants need to have decided what they want to research and share this information with us.

Every year: Winter Research Program: November - December. Spring Research Program: March - April. Summer Research Program: July - August.

YRJ is open to all high school students grade/class 8-12 (+gap year). We also accept entries from 7th graders who have yet to turn 13, as well as recent high school graduates who are taking a gap year and are not enrolled in university credits.

The registration fee can be paid at the last step of the registration form by bKash (just like online shopping). Our payment portal is secure and we don't save any details.

All registrants will receive an email from the YRJ team with the submission link a week before Research Manuscript. If you don't receive any email, kindly add info@yrjournal.org as a primary contact in your inbox and check your spam/promotions folder.

Every participant will receive a confirmation email post registration. Please check your junk/promotions tab. Post-registration if you still haven't receieved anything, please email notification@mail.yrjournal.org
